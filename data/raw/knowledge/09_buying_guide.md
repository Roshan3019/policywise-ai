# How to Choose the Right Car Insurance Policy — Buying Guide

Buying the right car insurance is not just about the lowest premium. This guide helps you evaluate policies systematically.

---

## Step 1: Decide the Policy Type

| Situation | Recommended Policy |
|---|---|
| Brand new car (0–3 years) | Comprehensive + Zero Dep + Engine Protection |
| Car 3–5 years old | Comprehensive + Zero Dep |
| Car 5–10 years old | Comprehensive without Zero Dep |
| Car older than 10 years | Third-Party only (often IDV too low to justify OD premium) |
| Already have long-term TP | Standalone OD policy |

---

## Step 2: Set the Right IDV

**Don't under-insure** (set IDV too low to reduce premium) — you'll receive an inadequate payout in case of total loss.

**Don't over-insure** (set IDV too high) — the insurer will still only pay the market value.

**Recommended:** Accept the insurer-calculated IDV or set it within ±10% of calculated value. Use an IDV calculator for your car's make, model, year, and variant.

---

## Step 3: Choose the Right Add-ons

**Priority add-ons for new cars:**
1. Zero Depreciation ✅ (must-have for first 3 years)
2. Engine Protection ✅ (must-have in flood-prone cities: Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata)
3. NCB Protect ✅ (if you have 35%+ NCB from previous vehicle)
4. Roadside Assistance ✅ (if you frequently do long drives)

**Add-ons to consider later:**
5. Invoice Cover (only for cars under 3 years)
6. Passenger PA (if you have regular co-passengers)
7. Key Replacement (for smart/remote key vehicles)

---

## Step 4: Evaluate the Insurer

Use these 4 metrics:

| Metric | Target | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR) | > 95% | IRDAI Annual Report |
| Network Garages | > 5,000 | Insurer website |
| Claim Settlement Speed | < 7 days for cashless | Customer reviews |
| Customer Support | 24/7 helpline | Self-verify |

**Avoid:** Insurers with CSR < 85% or unverified digital entities.

---

## Step 5: Compare Premiums Across Platforms

Use 3+ comparison platforms:
- Policybazaar.com
- Coverfox.com
- Acko.com (direct)
- Insurer's own website (may offer direct discount)

**Watch for hidden charges:**
- Processing fees
- Minimum add-on bundling
- Deductibles hidden in fine print

---

## Step 6: Understand Your Voluntary Deductible

Choosing a higher voluntary deductible reduces your premium. Calculate carefully:

**Example:**
- Extra voluntary deductible: ₹5,000 → 25% discount on OD premium
- OD premium (before discount): ₹12,000 → Save ₹3,000 per year
- But: Every claim costs you ₹5,000 extra out of pocket
- If you expect 1+ claims per year → deductible is not worth it

**Rule of thumb:** Choose voluntary deductible only if you're a careful driver with a clean record.

---

## Step 7: Read the Policy Wording

Before buying, always check:
- ✅ List of exclusions
- ✅ Claim intimation time limit (usually 24–48 hours)
- ✅ Geographic coverage limits
- ✅ Cashless garage network (is your preferred garage included?)
- ✅ Add-on specific terms (e.g., how many Zero Dep claims per year?)

---

## Step 8: Maintain Your Policy

After purchasing:
1. **Save the policy document** in a safe digital folder (Google Drive / email)
2. **Save the insurer's helpline number** in your phone
3. **Install the insurer's app** — most enable digital garage search and claim filing
4. **Carry a printout or soft copy** in your car at all times
5. **Set renewal reminder** 30–45 days before expiry

---

## Red Flags to Watch Out For

- Extremely low premium (often means underinsurance — low IDV)
- Insurer not in IRDAI list (check at **irdai.gov.in**)
- No physical or verifiable customer service
- Claim settlement ratio not publicly available
- Pressure to bundle with unnecessary add-ons

---

## Quick Checklist for First-Time Buyers

- [ ] Checked IRDAI list of registered insurers
- [ ] Selected appropriate policy type for car age
- [ ] IDV set correctly (not too low, not too high)
- [ ] Added Zero Dep (if car < 5 years)
- [ ] Added Engine Protection (if in flood-prone city)
- [ ] Verified CSR > 90%
- [ ] Checked cashless garage near home/office
- [ ] Read exclusions section
- [ ] Saved policy document and helpline number
