# Real-World Claim Scenarios — Q&A

This document answers the most common real-world insurance questions in a scenario-based format. Ideal for the AI chat assistant.

---

## Scenario 1: Flood / Waterlogging

**Q: My car was submerged in floodwater and won't start. Is this covered?**

A: Yes, if you have a **Comprehensive or Standalone OD** policy. Flood damage is covered under "Natural Calamities."

However:
- If you **drove through the waterlogged area knowingly** and the engine seized (hydrostatic lock / water ingression), the BASE policy may NOT cover engine damage.
- **Engine Protection add-on** specifically covers hydrostatic lock and internal engine damage from water ingression.
- The body damage, electrical systems, and interiors damaged by flooding are covered under the standard policy.

**What to do:** Do NOT try to start the car after flood damage (it can worsen hydrostatic lock). Call your insurer immediately and request a tow.

## Scenario 2: Accident — Own Fault

**Q: I accidentally hit a wall in my parking area. Can I claim?**

A: Yes. Accidental damage is covered under Comprehensive and Standalone OD policies, regardless of fault (your own accident included).

You will pay:
- Compulsory deductible (₹1,000 or ₹2,000)
- Depreciation on replaced parts (unless you have Zero Dep add-on)

**Note:** Filing a small claim (< ₹10,000) may result in losing your NCB. Calculate whether the claim is worth it: compare claim amount vs. NCB loss in next year's premium.

## Scenario 3: Hit and Run

**Q: Someone hit my parked car and drove away. Can I claim?**

A: Yes, if you have **Comprehensive insurance**.

**Steps:**
1. File an FIR at the nearest police station (mandatory for hit-and-run)
2. Photograph all damage
3. Notify your insurer within 24 hours
4. The insurer will assess damage and settle under your OD coverage

**Without Comprehensive coverage:** If you only have Third-Party insurance, you CANNOT claim for your own car's damage in a hit-and-run.

## Scenario 4: Car Theft

**Q: My car was stolen from outside my office. What do I do?**

A: Follow these steps immediately:

1. **File FIR** at the police station where the theft occurred — within 24 hours
2. **Call your insurer** — same day (most have 24/7 helplines)
3. **Submit documents:** FIR, all car keys, RC, policy, loan NOC if applicable
4. Wait for police to issue a **Non-Traceable Report (NTR)** — typically 60–90 days
5. Once NTR is received, insurer settles the claim for the **IDV amount**

**Covered under:** Comprehensive and Standalone OD policies (NOT third-party only).

## Scenario 5: Natural Disaster (Cyclone / Earthquake)

**Q: A cyclone damaged my car — roof dented, windshield shattered. Covered?**

A: Yes. Natural calamities including cyclones, typhoons, hurricanes, and earthquakes are covered under OD coverage.

**What's covered:**
- Body damage from storm debris
- Windshield and glass damage
- Electrical systems damaged by water ingression (if car was in the storm path)
- Flood damage alongside cyclone damage

**Note:** Glass/windshield damage alone (no other accident damage) can be claimed separately and may NOT affect NCB depending on your insurer's policy.

---

## Scenario 6: Fire

**Q: My car caught fire due to an electrical short circuit. Is it covered?**

A: Yes. Fire is a covered peril under both Comprehensive and Standalone OD policies.

**Covered:** Accidental fire, short circuit fire, lightning-struck fire.

**Not Covered:** If you intentionally set the car on fire (fraud), or if there is evidence of arson for insurance fraud.

**What to do:** Call fire brigade and emergency services first. Then file FIR (if fire was suspicious). Notify insurer immediately.

## Scenario 7: Third-Party Accident (You Were at Fault)

**Q: I accidentally crashed into another car and caused ₹80,000 damage to it. Who pays?**

A: Your **Third-Party liability coverage** pays.

- TP property damage: Up to ₹7.5 lakh per incident
- TP bodily injury/death: No limit (determined by Motor Accident Claims Tribunal)

**Process:** The third party files a case in the MACT (Motor Accident Claims Tribunal). Your insurer represents you and pays the awarded amount.

---

## Scenario 8: Accident — Not Your Fault

**Q: Another car ran into mine at a signal. They admitted fault. Can I get a claim?**

A: You have two options:

**Option A — Claim on YOUR policy (Own Damage):**
- Faster and simpler
- Your insurer settles, then recovers from third party's insurer
- You pay deductible + potential NCB loss
- Depreciation deducted unless you have Zero Dep

**Option B — Claim from Third Party's insurer (TP Claim):**
- No NCB loss on your policy
- No deductible
- But process is longer (may take months via MACT)
- Requires FIR, proof of fault
- Best option if the damage is large and fault is clear

## Scenario 9: Parking Damage (Scratches/Dents)

**Q: Someone scratched my car in a parking lot. Can I claim?**

A: Yes, under OD coverage. However, consider carefully before claiming small amounts.

**Practical advice:**
- If repair cost is ₹8,000 and your NCB discount saves you ₹12,000 per year → Don't claim! Pay out of pocket.
- If repair cost is ₹60,000 and you have Zero Dep + NCB Protect → Definitely claim.

The **NCB Protect add-on** makes small claims worthwhile since NCB is preserved.

---

## Scenario 10: Driving Someone Else's Car

**Q: I borrowed my friend's car and had an accident. Whose insurance applies?**

A: The **car's insurance policy** (your friend's policy) covers the accident — not your own policy.

**Important conditions:**
- You must have a valid driving licence
- The friend must have authorised you to drive
- You must not be driving for commercial purposes

If your friend has Comprehensive insurance, the OD claim is covered. The insurer may raise a dispute if the car was lent frequently (usage pattern changes).

---

## Scenario 11: Drunk Driving Accident

**Q: My friend was driving my car drunk and had an accident. Will insurance pay?**

A: **No.** Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs is an absolute exclusion. Neither OD nor TP claims will be settled.

Additionally:
- Your policy could be **cancelled**
- The driver may face criminal charges
- You as the owner may face legal liability

---

## Scenario 12: Learner's Licence Driver Accident

**Q: My son has a learner's licence and drove alone and had an accident. Is it covered?**

A: **No.** A learner's licence holder must be accompanied by a valid licence holder while driving. If the learner drove alone, the policy conditions are violated, and the claim will be rejected.
