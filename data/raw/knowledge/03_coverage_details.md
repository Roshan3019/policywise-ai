# Car Insurance Coverage Details — What's Covered and What's Not

## Own Damage Coverage

### What is Own Damage (OD)?
Own Damage coverage protects your car against physical loss or damage caused to your own vehicle. It is available under Comprehensive and Standalone OD policies.

### Perils Covered Under Own Damage

#### 1. Accidental Damage
- Collision with another vehicle
- Overturning of the vehicle
- Damage from falling objects (tree branches, poles)
- Impact damage from hitting a wall or barrier

#### 2. Fire and Explosion
- Fire due to accident or short circuit
- Explosion of the vehicle's engine or fuel tank
- Lightning strike damage

#### 3. Natural Calamities
- Floods and inundation (the most common major claim in India)
- Earthquake and earth subsidence
- Cyclone, typhoon, hurricane
- Landslide and rockslide
- Hailstorm

#### 4. Man-Made Disasters
- Riots and civil unrest
- Malicious acts and vandalism
- Terrorist activity (subject to specific endorsement)
- Strike-related damage

#### 5. Theft
- Theft of the entire vehicle
- Theft while the car is unattended

#### 6. Transit Damage
- Damage while the car is being transported by rail, road, air, or inland waterway

## Third-Party Coverage

### What is Third-Party (TP) Coverage?
Third-Party coverage protects you against legal liability arising from damage or injury caused to another person or their property while driving your car.

### What TP Covers

#### Bodily Injury to Third Party
- Medical expenses for third parties injured in an accident
- Compensation for permanent disability
- Death compensation to the family of the deceased

**Settlement:** Through the MACT (Motor Accident Claims Tribunal) system. No upper limit on the claim amount — determined by the tribunal based on income, age, and circumstances.

#### Property Damage to Third Party
- Damage to another vehicle
- Damage to a building, shop front, or wall
- Damage to public property (street lights, dividers)

**Limit:** ₹7.5 lakh maximum per accident (as per IRDAI cap on single TP property damage)

## Personal Accident Cover (PA Cover)

### Mandatory PA Cover for Owner-Driver
Since January 2019, IRDAI made PA cover mandatory for all car owners:
- **Sum Insured:** ₹15 lakh
- **Covers:** Death and permanent total disability
- **Premium:** ₹750 per year (standalone PA cover)

### Compensation Table (Owner-Driver)
| Nature of Injury | % of Sum Insured Paid |
|---|---|
| Death | 100% |
| Total and irrecoverable loss of both eyes or limbs | 100% |
| Loss of one eye AND one limb | 100% |
| Loss of one limb or one eye | 50% |
| Permanent total paralysis | 100% |

### PA Cover for Paid Driver
Separate PA cover for a paid driver: typically ₹2 lakh sum insured.

### PA Cover for Passengers
Can be purchased as an add-on to cover all unnamed passengers in the vehicle.

## Standard Exclusions (What's NOT Covered)

### Absolute Exclusions (can never be covered)
1. **Driving under influence:** Any accident while under alcohol or drugs
2. **Unlicensed driving:** Driving without a valid licence (or learner's licence without a valid driver beside)
3. **War and nuclear risk:** Damage from war, invasion, nuclear weapons
4. **Intentional damage:** Deliberately damaging your own car
5. **Outside India:** Use beyond India's territorial boundaries (exception: Nepal, Bangladesh, Bhutan with prior endorsement)

### Conditional Exclusions (can be mitigated with add-ons)
1. **Depreciation on parts:** Mitigated by Zero Depreciation add-on
2. **Engine damage from waterlogging:** Mitigated by Engine & Gearbox Protection add-on
3. **Tyre damage (without vehicle damage):** Cannot be claimed alone
4. **Consequential loss:** Loss from delay in repair

### Mechanical/Electrical Breakdown
- Normal wear and tear Not covered under any plan
- Mechanical breakdown Not covered (use manufacturer warranty)
- Electrical failures due to ageing Not covered

## Coverage for CNG / LPG Vehicles

- CNG or LPG kits factory-fitted are automatically covered
- Aftermarket CNG/LPG kits MUST be declared and separately endorsed
- Failure to declare voids the claim on the kit damage
- Endorsement for a ₹20,000 CNG kit costs approximately ₹100–₹200 extra in premium

---

## Geographical Limits

Standard Indian policies cover:
- ✅ All of India
- ✅ Nepal, Bangladesh, Bhutan (with prior written endorsement)
- ❌ Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and all other countries (not covered)
