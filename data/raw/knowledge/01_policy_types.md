# Car Insurance Policy Types in India

## Overview

There are three main types of car insurance policies available in India, each offering different levels of coverage. Understanding the differences is essential to choosing the right policy for your needs and budget.

---

## 1. Third-Party Liability Insurance

### What It Covers
- **Bodily injury or death** caused to a third party (pedestrian, passenger in another car)
- **Property damage** caused to a third party's vehicle or property
- **Personal Accident Cover** for the owner-driver (mandatory: ₹15 lakh sum insured)

### What It Does NOT Cover
- ❌ Damage to your own car (from accident, fire, flood, theft)
- ❌ Damage caused to your car by natural disasters

### Premium
- Fixed by IRDAI annually based on engine capacity
- Most affordable option

### Engine Capacity → Annual Premium (2024 IRDAI rates)
| Engine CC | Annual TP Premium |
|---|---|
| Up to 1000cc | ₹2,094 |
| 1000–1500cc | ₹3,416 |
| 1500cc and above | ₹7,897 |

### Who Should Choose This?
- Very old cars with low market value
- Cars used rarely / stored long-term
- Owners who accept the risk of self-funding own car repairs

### Legal Requirement
✅ **Mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.** Every vehicle on Indian roads must have at minimum a third-party insurance policy.

---

## 2. Standalone Own Damage (OD) Insurance

### What It Covers
- **Accidental damage** to your own car
- **Fire and explosion** damage to your car
- **Natural disasters:** floods, earthquakes, cyclones, landslides
- **Man-made disasters:** riots, strikes, terrorist activity
- **Theft** of the vehicle (subject to waiting period)
- **In-transit damage** (while transporting the car on a carrier)

### What It Does NOT Cover
- ❌ Third-party liabilities (you need a separate TP policy)
- ❌ Damage from driving under the influence of alcohol/drugs
- ❌ Mechanical or electrical breakdown
- ❌ Damage beyond normal wear and tear

### Premium
- Calculated as a percentage of IDV
- Standard OD rate: 2–5% of IDV depending on car age

### Important Note
A Standalone OD policy **cannot be purchased alone** — you must independently have a valid Third-Party policy for the same vehicle.

### Who Should Choose This?
- Car owners who already have a long-term TP policy
- Owners wanting to switch OD insurer without changing TP insurer

---

## 3. Comprehensive Insurance (Package Policy)

### What It Covers
Combines **both** Third-Party + Own Damage coverage:

- ✅ Own damage (accident, fire, natural disaster, theft)
- ✅ Third-party bodily injury and property damage
- ✅ Personal accident cover for owner-driver
- ✅ Add-ons can be attached (Zero Dep, Engine Protection, NCB Protect, etc.)

### What It Does NOT Cover
- ❌ Mechanical/electrical breakdown
- ❌ Damage due to drunk driving
- ❌ Depreciation on parts (unless Zero Dep add-on purchased)
- ❌ Consequential loss (loss caused by delay in repair)
- ❌ Damage to tyres and tubes unless the vehicle is also damaged

### Premium
Most expensive of the three, but offers the most complete protection.

Premium = Third-Party Premium + Own Damage Premium − NCB discount + Add-on premiums

### Who Should Choose This?
- New car owners (first 3–5 years)
- Owners of expensive or luxury vehicles
- Those living in flood-prone or theft-prone areas
- Anyone wanting complete peace of mind

| Feature | Third-Party | Standalone OD | Comprehensive |

## Long-Term Policies

IRDAI now mandates:
- **3-year Third-Party** policy for new cars at the time of purchase
- **5-year Third-Party** policy for new two-wheelers

For comprehensive coverage on new cars, owners typically buy:
- 3-year TP + 1-year OD (the OD can be switched annually)
