# Car Insurance Renewal Guide

## Why Timely Renewal Matters

Your car insurance policy expires on the last day of its validity period. Driving with an expired policy is the same as driving without insurance — it is illegal under the Motor Vehicles Act and you are fully liable for any accident-related costs.

---

## When to Renew

- **Best time to renew:** 30 days before expiry (gives time if there are issues)
- **Latest deadline:** On or before the expiry date for seamless continuation
- **Grace period:** A 30-day grace period exists, but driving during this window is at your own risk

---

## Grace Period — What It Means

A 30-day grace period is given after policy expiry. During this period:
- You can renew the policy without penalty
- **But:** If you have an accident during the grace period and haven't renewed → claim will be rejected (the policy is expired)
- The grace period is only for administrative renewals, NOT a coverage extension

**Recommendation:** Never rely on the grace period. Renew before expiry.

---

## NCB and Renewal

### NCB Continuity
- If you renew **within the grace period (30 days):** NCB is preserved
- If you renew **after 90 days of expiry:** NCB is **forfeited** — you lose all accumulated discounts
- Between 30–90 days: Some insurers preserve NCB; check your policy terms

### NCB Certificate
When switching insurers:
- Request an **NCB Certificate** from your current insurer at the time of renewal
- Submit this certificate to the new insurer to transfer your NCB
- NCB belongs to you (the owner) — not the vehicle

---

## Break-in Insurance / Lapsed Policy

### What is Break-in Insurance?
When the policy lapses (expired and not renewed within the grace period), it becomes a "break-in" case.

### Impact of Policy Break-in
1. **NCB Loss:** All NCB accumulated is forfeited
2. **Fresh Inspection Required:** The insurer sends a surveyor to inspect the vehicle before reinstating coverage
3. **Higher Premium:** May be loaded with additional premium
4. **Claim Void Period:** Some insurers have a waiting period before claims can be filed after break-in renewal

### Break-in Renewal Process
1. Contact your insurer or new insurer
2. Submit vehicle for physical inspection
3. Pay the computed premium (without NCB benefit)
4. Policy is reinstated after inspection approval

---

## Can You Switch Insurers at Renewal?

**Yes.** You are free to switch to any other IRDAI-registered insurer at renewal. This is one of the most consumer-friendly aspects of the system.

**Transferring NCB When Switching:**
1. In the last week of your current policy, apply for an NCB Certificate from your current insurer
2. Most insurers provide this online (download from portal)
3. Submit the certificate to your new insurer
4. The new insurer applies the same NCB percentage

**Caution:** Always ensure the new policy starts the day your old policy ends — no gap in coverage.

---

## Online Renewal Process (Recommended)

Most modern insurers allow 100% digital renewal:

1. Go to insurer website or use an aggregator (Policybazaar, Coverfox, etc.)
2. Enter your vehicle registration number (RC)
3. The system auto-populates vehicle details
4. Choose coverage type, IDV, and add-ons
5. Quote generated instantly
6. Pay via UPI / netbanking / card
7. Policy document emailed immediately
8. Soft copy is valid — no physical document required for traffic police per MV Act 2019

---

## Renewal Premium Calculation

Your renewal premium may change year-on-year due to:

| Factor | Direction | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| IDV decreases each year (vehicle ages) | ↓ | Lower OD premium |
| NCB increases (if no claim) | ↑ | More discount = Lower net |
| IRDAI revises TP rates annually | ↑/↓ | TP component changes |
| New add-ons added | ↑ | Higher premium |

---

## Long-Term Policy Considerations

For vehicles newer than 3 years:
- The **3-year TP** policy cannot be cancelled easily
- You can still switch the **OD** portion annually after year 1
- Switching OD insurer at year 2 or 3 is common and legal

---

## Reminder Tips

1. Set a calendar reminder 45 days before expiry
2. Review add-ons at every renewal — your needs change
3. Check IRDAI's annual CSR report before switching
4. Never let the policy lapse — even for a single day
5. Compare quotes on 3 platforms before renewing
