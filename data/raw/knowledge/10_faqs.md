# Car Insurance — Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

This document contains the most commonly asked questions about car insurance in India, with clear, accurate answers.

---

**Q1: Is car insurance mandatory in India?**

A: Yes. The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 makes it compulsory to have at least Third-Party Liability insurance for any vehicle plying on Indian public roads. Driving without valid insurance is punishable with a fine of ₹2,000 (first offence) or ₹4,000 (repeat offence) and/or imprisonment.

---

**Q2: What is the difference between Third-Party and Comprehensive insurance?**

A: Third-Party insurance only covers damage/injury caused to other people or their property. It does NOT cover your own car. Comprehensive insurance covers both — your own car's damage (from accident, flood, theft, fire) AND third-party liabilities. Comprehensive is more expensive but provides complete protection.

---

**Q3: What is No Claim Bonus (NCB) and how does it work?**

A: NCB is a discount earned on your Own Damage premium for every claim-free policy year. It starts at 20% after year 1 and goes up to 50% after 5+ claim-free years. Making even one claim resets NCB to 0%, unless you have an NCB Protect add-on. NCB belongs to you (the policyholder), not the car — so you can transfer it when you buy a new car.

---

**Q4: What is IDV and why does it matter?**

A: IDV (Insured Declared Value) is the market value of your car after depreciation. It is the maximum amount the insurer pays if your car is stolen or declared a total loss. A higher IDV means better protection but a higher premium. Setting IDV too low to save on premium is a common mistake — it results in undercompensation during a total loss claim.

---

**Q5: What is Zero Depreciation insurance? Do I need it?**

A: Zero Depreciation (also called Nil Dep or Bumper-to-Bumper) is an add-on that ensures the insurer pays the full cost of replaced parts without deducting depreciation. Without it, plastic parts (bumpers, mirrors) face 50% depreciation deduction. For new cars (under 5 years), Zero Dep is strongly recommended — the premium extra is worth it compared to the savings on claims.

---

**Q6: What happens if I don't renew my policy on time?**

A: Your policy lapses. Driving with an expired policy is illegal. If you renew within 30 days (grace period), you can usually retain your NCB. If you renew after 90 days, you lose your NCB. After expiry, a physical inspection of the vehicle is required before renewal can be processed — a break-in renewal.

---

**Q7: Can I switch insurers at the time of renewal?**

A: Yes! You are completely free to switch to any IRDAI-registered insurer each year at renewal. Obtain an NCB Certificate from your current insurer and submit it to the new one to transfer your accumulated NCB. Compare premiums on aggregator platforms like Policybazaar before switching.

---

**Q8: My car was damaged in a flood. Is it covered?**

A: Yes, if you have Comprehensive or Standalone OD insurance. Flood is covered under Natural Calamities. However, if you drove into a flooded area knowingly and the engine seized (hydrostatic lock), the base policy may not cover the engine damage — you need the Engine Protection add-on for that. Do NOT try to start a flood-submerged car — it makes the engine damage worse.

---

**Q9: What do I do immediately after a car accident?**

A: (1) Ensure safety and call emergency services if needed. (2) Photograph all damage and the scene. (3) Note third-party vehicle number. (4) File FIR if there are injuries, death, or hit-and-run. (5) Notify your insurer within 24 hours via their helpline. (6) Do not start repairs until the surveyor inspects.

---

**Q10: Can I claim insurance for engine repair?**

A: Standard car insurance does not cover mechanical or electrical breakdown, including engine failure from wear and tear. However, engine damage caused by an accident, fire, or water ingression (flooding) IS covered. The Engine Protection add-on extends coverage to hydrostatic lock and internal engine damage from water/oil leakage.

---

**Q11: What is a cashless claim?**

A: A cashless claim is settled directly between your insurer and the network garage — you don't need to pay for repairs upfront (except the deductible). Your car must be repaired at an insurer-empanelled garage. It's faster and more convenient than reimbursement claims.

---

**Q12: How is car insurance premium calculated?**

A: Your premium has two parts: (1) Third-Party premium — fixed by IRDAI based on engine capacity. (2) Own Damage premium — calculated as a percentage of your IDV. Add-on premiums add to the total. Discounts include NCB (up to 50%), voluntary deductible, and anti-theft device. GST at 18% applies on the total.

---

**Q13: I borrowed a friend's car and had an accident. Whose insurance applies?**

A: The car's insurance policy (your friend's) applies — not your personal one. You must have a valid driving licence, and the friend must have authorised you to use the car. If the friend has comprehensive insurance, OD damage is covered.

---

**Q14: What is Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR) and why is it important?**

A: CSR is the percentage of claims an insurer settles out of all claims received in a year. Example: a CSR of 97% means 97 of 100 claims were settled. Always choose an insurer with CSR > 90% (ideally > 95%) — it directly predicts how likely your claim will be approved. IRDAI publishes CSR data annually.

---

**Q15: Does car insurance cover theft of items left inside the car?**

A: No. Car insurance covers the theft of the entire vehicle, not personal belongings left inside. For personal items (laptop, camera, etc.), you need a separate home/travel insurance policy that covers portable belongings.

---

**Q16: Is CNG kit covered by car insurance?**

A: Factory-fitted CNG kits are automatically covered. If you install an aftermarket CNG kit, you MUST declare it and add it to your policy via an endorsement. Failure to declare an aftermarket CNG kit means the kit damage will not be covered in a claim.

---

**Q17: What is an endorsement in car insurance?**

A: An endorsement is a written amendment to your policy document. Common endorsements include: adding a CNG kit, changing registered address, correcting the name spelling, adding a spouse as a driver, or changing the vehicle's purpose of use. Endorsements can add or reduce premium depending on the change.

---

**Q18: Can I buy car insurance without having the physical car inspected?**

A: Yes, for a new or recently insured car — you can purchase online without inspection. But for a lapsed policy (break-in renewal) or switching insurers mid-term, most insurers require a physical or video inspection before coverage begins. Some digital-first insurers accept photo-based inspections.

---

**Q19: What is the Insurance Ombudsman and when do I use it?**

A: The Insurance Ombudsman is an independent authority that resolves disputes between policyholders and insurers. Use it if your claim is rejected unfairly and the insurer hasn't resolved your complaint within 30 days. There are 17 Ombudsman offices across India. The service is free for policyholders.

---

**Q20: What is telematics-based car insurance?**

A: Telematics insurance (also called "Pay-As-You-Drive" or "Pay-How-You-Drive") uses a device or app to monitor your driving behaviour — speed, braking, time of day, distance. Safe driving habits result in lower premiums. Example: Tata AIG's Drive Smart, Bajaj Allianz's Motor OTS. Ideal for careful drivers who cover low mileage.
