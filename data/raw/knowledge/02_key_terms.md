# Key Terms in Car Insurance — Complete Glossary

Understanding these terms is essential before buying, renewing, or claiming car insurance in India.

---

## IDV — Insured Declared Value

**Definition:** The maximum amount the insurer will pay if your car is stolen or declared a total loss.

**How IDV is Calculated:**
```
IDV = Manufacturer's Listed Selling Price − Depreciation
```

**Depreciation Schedule (IRDAI Guidelines):**
| Car Age | Depreciation % |
|---|---|
| Less than 6 months | 5% |
| 6 months to 1 year | 15% |
| 1 to 2 years | 20% |
| 2 to 3 years | 30% |
| 3 to 4 years | 40% |
| 4 to 5 years | 50% |

**Key Points:**
- Higher IDV = Higher premium but better protection
- Lower IDV = Lower premium but insufficient claim payout
- IDV is negotiable within ±15% of the calculated value
- All accessories not factory-fitted must be separately declared

---

## NCB — No Claim Bonus

**Definition:** A discount on the Own Damage premium for each claim-free year.

**NCB Scale:**
| Claim-Free Years | NCB Discount |
|---|---|
| 1 year | 20% |
| 2 years | 25% |
| 3 years | 35% |
| 4 years | 45% |
| 5+ years | 50% |

**Key Points:**
- NCB is earned by the policyholder (not the vehicle) — it transfers when you buy a new car
- Even one claim resets NCB to 0%
- NCB can be protected with the **NCB Protect add-on**
- NCB certificate must be obtained when switching insurers

---

## Premium

**Definition:** The amount you pay to the insurer to maintain your policy. Paid annually (or monthly/quarterly with some insurers).

**Components of Premium:**
1. **Third-Party Premium:** Fixed by IRDAI based on engine capacity
2. **Own Damage Premium:** Calculated as a % of IDV
3. **Add-on Premiums:** Each add-on costs extra
4. **GST:** 18% GST on the total premium

**Discounts That Reduce Premium:**
- NCB discount (up to 50%)
- ARAI-approved anti-theft device discount (2.5%)
- Voluntary deductible discount
- AAI membership discount (5%)

---

## Deductible (Compulsory + Voluntary)

**Compulsory Deductible:**
The fixed amount the policyholder must pay for every claim:
- Vehicles up to 1500cc: ₹1,000
- Vehicles above 1500cc: ₹2,000

**Voluntary Deductible:**
An additional amount you agree to pay over the compulsory deductible in exchange for a lower premium.

| Voluntary Deductible | Premium Discount |
|---|---|
| ₹2,500 | 20% |
| ₹5,000 | 25% |
| ₹7,500 | 30% |
| ₹15,000 | 35% |

**Important:** Higher voluntary deductible reduces your premium but increases your out-of-pocket cost per claim.

---

## Zero Depreciation (Nil Depreciation / Bumper-to-Bumper)

**Definition:** An add-on that removes the depreciation deduction from parts replaced during a claim.

**Without Zero Dep:** Insurer pays full cost minus depreciation (e.g., rubber parts depreciate 50%)

**With Zero Dep:** Insurer pays the full cost of replacement parts

**Eligible:** Cars up to 5 years old (some insurers allow up to 7 years)

---

## Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR)

**Definition:** The percentage of claims settled by an insurer out of all claims received in a financial year.

```
CSR = (Claims Settled / Claims Received) × 100
```

**Example:** HDFC ERGO settled 13,07,722 claims out of 13,52,116 → CSR = 96.74%

**IRDAI publishes CSR annually.** Always choose an insurer with CSR > 90%.

---

## Cashless Claim

**Definition:** A claim where the insurer directly pays the repair cost to the network garage. You pay only the deductible.

**Requirement:** Repair must be done at an insurer's empanelled (network) garage.

---

## Reimbursement Claim

**Definition:** You pay the repair cost upfront at any garage, then submit bills to the insurer for reimbursement.

**Use When:** The nearest network garage is far away, or in an emergency.

---

## Endorsement

**Definition:** A written amendment to an insurance policy to add, remove, or modify coverage.

**Examples:**
- Adding a new CNG kit to an existing policy
- Changing the registered address of the vehicle
- Including a spouse as an additional driver
- Correcting errors in the policy document

---

## Total Loss

**Definition:** When the cost to repair a car exceeds 75% of its IDV, the insurer declares it a "total loss" (constructive total loss) or "actual total loss" (completely destroyed).

**Settlement:** Insurer pays the IDV amount. The salvaged vehicle goes to the insurer.

---

## Float / Grace Period

**Definition:** A short period (typically 30 days) after the policy expiry date during which the policy can be renewed without penalty.

**After Grace Period:** The policy lapses. Renewal requires a fresh inspection and NCB is lost.

---

## Personal Accident Cover (PA Cover)

**Definition:** Compensation paid in case the owner-driver is injured or killed in an accident.

**Mandatory PA Cover:** ₹15 lakh sum insured for owner-driver (mandatory since 2018 by IRDAI).

**Optional PA:** Additional cover for paid drivers and passengers.

---

## Exclusions (Common)

Things NOT covered under standard car insurance:
1. Driving under influence of alcohol or drugs
2. Driving without a valid driving licence
3. Mechanical or electrical breakdown
4. Damage to tyres/tubes unless vehicle is also damaged
5. War, nuclear risks, or intentional damage
6. Consequential losses
7. Normal wear and tear / ageing
8. Use of vehicle outside geographical limits (except Nepal, Bangladesh, Bhutan)
