# Car Insurance Claim Process — Step-by-Step Guide

Understanding how to file a claim correctly is as important as choosing the right policy. An improperly filed claim can be rejected even for valid incidents.

---

## Immediate Steps After an Accident

### Do These First (Within Minutes)

1. **Stay safe:** Move your car to a safe spot if possible
2. **Call emergency services:** Dial 112 for police and 108 for ambulance if there are injuries
3. **Photograph everything:**
   - Damage to your car (all angles)
   - Damage to the third party's vehicle or property
   - Location, road conditions, signage
   - Number plates of all vehicles involved
4. **Collect information:** Third-party driver's name, vehicle number, insurance details
5. **File an FIR:** At the nearest police station (mandatory for theft, serious injuries, hit-and-run)
6. **Notify your insurer:** Call the insurer's 24/7 helpline immediately — delay can jeopardise your claim

---

## Claim Process — Cashless Claims

Cashless claims are settled directly between the insurer and the empanelled garage. **You pay nothing** (except the compulsory deductible and depreciation on parts if no Zero Dep add-on).

### Step-by-Step Cashless Claim

**Step 1: Intimate the Insurer**
- Call the insurer helpline (available 24/7)
- Provide: Policy number, vehicle number, date/time/location of incident, brief description
- Get a claim registration number

**Step 2: Vehicle Inspection**
- Insurer dispatches a surveyor to inspect the damage (within 48 hours)
- The surveyor assesses the extent of damage and estimates repair cost
- Do NOT start any repairs before the surveyor arrives (unless emergency)

**Step 3: Select a Network Garage**
- Take the car to any cashless network garage (insurer provides list/app)
- The garage submits a repair estimate to the insurer

**Step 4: Approval and Repair**
- Insurer approves the estimate (may negotiate with garage)
- Repairs begin; spare parts replaced as approved

**Step 5: Settlement**
- Insurer pays the garage directly for approved repair amount
- You pay: Compulsory deductible + Depreciation on parts (unless Zero Dep) + Any non-approved items

**Step 6: Take Delivery**
- Sign the satisfaction note from the garage
- Collect all documents (repair invoice, replaced parts list)

## Claim Process — Reimbursement Claims

Use when no network garage is accessible or in an emergency situation.

### Step-by-Step Reimbursement Claim

**Step 1: Intimate the Insurer** (Same as cashless — call immediately)

**Step 2: Inform About Non-Network Garage**
- Get verbal/written approval from the insurer to proceed at a non-network garage

**Step 3: Vehicle Repair**
- Get the car repaired at any garage
- Keep ALL original bills, invoices, and part replacement receipts

**Step 4: Submit Claim Documents**
- Policy document copy
- Driving licence copy
- Vehicle RC (Registration Certificate) copy
- FIR copy (if applicable)
- Original repair invoices and bills
- Original spare parts receipts
- Duly filled and signed claim form

**Step 5: Surveyor Inspection**
- For reimbursement, surveyor may inspect before or after repair
- Submit photos of damage taken before repair begins

**Step 6: Claim Settlement**
- Insurer reviews and approves the claim amount (after deducting depreciation, deductible)
- Amount credited to your registered bank account within 7–14 working days

## Theft Claim Process

**Step 1:** File FIR at police station **immediately**
**Step 2:** Notify insurer same day
**Step 3:** Submit documents:
- Original FIR copy
- All sets of car keys (original must be surrendered)
- Vehicle RC and policy document
- NOC from financier if car is on loan
- Form 28, 29, 30 (vehicle transfer documents)

**Step 4:** Police Investigation
- Wait for police to issue a Non-Traceable Report (NTR) — typically 60–90 days

**Step 5:** Settlement
- Insurer pays IDV after receiving NTR
- Insurer retains the salvage rights

## Common Reasons for Claim Rejection

1. **Not notifying the insurer on time** — Delay in reporting can void the claim
2. **Driving without a valid licence** — Automatic rejection
3. **Driving under influence** — Claim is void
4. **Policy lapsed** — No valid insurance at the time of incident
5. **Vehicle used for commercial purposes** — If private car used for hire/delivery
6. **Mechanical breakdown claimed as accident** — Not covered
7. **Intentional damage** — Fraud, will be rejected and may lead to legal action
8. **Incorrect information** — Errors in claim form (wrong date, location)

## Claim Status Tracking

- All major insurers provide online claim tracking
- Use the claim reference number provided at intimation
- IRDAI Bima Bharosa portal for escalation: **bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in**
- Insurance Ombudsman: For unresolved claim disputes

| Reimbursement claim | Within 30 days of document submission |
